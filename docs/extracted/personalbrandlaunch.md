## Hooks

### 1. Always include a human face in your hook (appeared 3 times) [HIGH confidence]
**Insight:** Human faces in hooks dramatically outperform faceless content—the difference can be 10K vs 10M views on identical content. Studies show viewers' eyes naturally dart to human faces faster than any other visual element when scrolling, making face-forward hooks essential for stopping the scroll.

**Evidence:**
- "Studies show humans spot real people's faces way quicker than animal faces or random stuff like cars or buildings, and are eyes dart to human faces faster than any other animal or object. Which means when scrolling, you're more likely to pause on a human face than any animal or object. So keep in mind, always have a face in Hooks."
- "It's the same person, niche, topic, account, voice, even identical editing... But there is one main difference between the 10K view video and the 10 million view video... always have a face in Hooks."
- "Studies show humans spot real people's faces way quicker than animal faces or random stuff like cars or buildings, and are eyes dart to human faces faster than any other animal or object."

### 2. Lead with specific, quantified personal results rather than generic advice (appeared 3 times) [HIGH confidence]
**Insight:** Million-view hooks use concrete numbers and outcomes (e.g., '30 pounds in 4 months', 'SAT 1270 to 1580') rather than abstract principles. Specificity creates immediate relevance and curiosity across broader audiences because demonstrated results are more compelling than universal statements.

**Evidence:**
- "100 view hook: 'weight loss is not about cardio' vs 1 million view hook: 'I lost over 30 pounds in four months, and here are the five things that I would do if I were to start all over'"
- "1 million view hook example: 'Here's how I dropped my SAT score from a 1270 to 1580 in just one month, and how that score opened doors to Harvard, Stanford, Yale, Duke, UPenn, Rice, and more'"
- "'100 view hooks make one person care, but 1 million view hooks make anyone care'"

### 3. Use multiple contrasting identity angles to create curiosity (appeared 2 times) [HIGH confidence]
**Insight:** Lead with several contrasting personal attributes (achievements, lifestyle quirks, milestones) to appeal to different audience segments and create multi-dimensional intrigue.

**Evidence:**

- "Lead with multiple contrasting identity angles (achievements, lifestyle quirks, personal milestones) to create curiosity and appeal to different audience segments."

**Evidence:**

Most creators should use a strategic mix across all three stages.
